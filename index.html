Case Study

How Influencer Strategy
Moved the Revenue Needle

The Brand

Whey91 — Health Supplements

A growing health supplement brand competing in a saturated market dominated by legacy players with large ad budgets. The challenge: drive meaningful sales growth without a mega-budget.

The Challenge

Break Through the Noise

Traditional paid ads were underperforming. The brand needed authentic reach — the kind that drives real purchase intent, not just impressions. Trust was the missing ingredient.

My Role

Account Manager & Strategist

I owned the strategy end-to-end: influencer identification, content brief development, AI-assisted creative optimisation, paid amplification of organic content, and performance reporting.

The Strategy

Micro-Influencer Stack + AI Optimisation

Instead of one big name, I built a stack of 15–20 micro-influencers (50k–300k followers) whose audiences had genuine fitness intent. Used AI tools to analyse content performance patterns and brief creators on the exact hooks, formats, and CTAs most likely to convert in this category.

Amplification

Paid Boost on Proven Organic

Instead of creating fresh paid creative, I identified the top-performing influencer posts using engagement-to-reach ratios and boosted those directly via Meta Ads. This meant we were spending on content already proven to resonate — eliminating creative risk and reducing CPAs significantly.

Outcome

Compounding Growth Over Campaign Window

The influencer content kept generating organic reach and sales long after the paid period ended, creating a compounding effect that traditional ads can't replicate. The campaign became the brand's new benchmark.

68%
Sales growth delivered through a combination of micro-influencer authenticity, AI-assisted content briefing, and strategic paid amplification of proven organic creative — at a fraction of what a traditional celebrity campaign would cost.
